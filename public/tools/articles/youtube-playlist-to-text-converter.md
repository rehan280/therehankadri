You've got a YouTube playlist. Now you want it in a usable format — a numbered list, a clean text doc, a CSV you can drop into a spreadsheet.

Copy-pasting from YouTube into a doc manually is painful. Formatting gets broken. Numbers don't align. You spend 10 minutes cleaning up what should have taken 30 seconds.

This tool takes your pasted playlist content and formats it cleanly in one step.

## When you need playlist content in text format

More often than you might think:

**Course outlines.** If you're creating a YouTube course series, converting your playlist to a numbered list gives you an instant syllabus you can share with viewers, put on a landing page, or include in a welcome email.

**Research documentation.** When you're compiling a resource list for a blog post, newsletter, or audience recommendation, a clean numbered playlist is ready to paste.

**Content planning.** Exporting your planned video series as text lets you reorder, edit, and share it in tools like Notion, Google Docs, or Trello.

**Study notes.** Students building personal study playlists sometimes want a text version to track progress, annotate, or share.

## How to use the playlist to text converter

**Step 1:** Go to the YouTube playlist you want to convert. Copy the video titles and any other details you want to capture from the playlist page.

**Step 2:** Paste the copied content into the converter tool.

**Step 3:** Choose your output format:
- Clean numbered list (1. Video Title, 2. Video Title...)
- Plain text (one line per video, no numbers)
- CSV format (comma-separated, importable to spreadsheets)

**Step 4:** Copy the formatted output or download it.

## What the output formats are good for

**Numbered list:** Best for syllabus pages, blog post resources sections, and share-ready content recommendations.

**Plain text:** Best for pasting into notes apps, emails, or tools where you'll apply your own formatting.

**CSV:** Best for spreadsheets and databases. Import into Google Sheets, Airtable, or Notion databases where you want to add columns for notes, completion status, or priority.

## Frequently asked questions

**Does this tool pull playlist data directly from YouTube?**
You paste the content manually. This keeps it simple and doesn't require API access or login.

**Can I add additional columns in CSV mode?**
The tool outputs a basic CSV. Once you import it into a spreadsheet, you can add as many columns as you need.

**Is there a video limit for the conversion?**
No limit. Paste playlists of any size.

**Can I convert a playlist with timestamps or durations included?**
If your pasted content includes timestamps or durations, the converter formats those alongside the titles.

**What's the best format for a blog post resource section?**
Numbered list. It's clean, scannable, and familiar to readers.
