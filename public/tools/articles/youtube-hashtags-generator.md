## How hashtags actually work on YouTube

Hashtags on YouTube serve two functions most people don't know about.

First, they show up clickably in your video description. Viewers who tap one see a hashtag-browse page showing all videos using that tag. That's free discovery.

Second, for YouTube Shorts specifically, hashtags help the algorithm understand what your content is about before viewer data comes in. A new Shorts video has zero watch history. Hashtags give the algorithm a starting point.

Here's why that matters: YouTube's short-form algorithm uses topic signals to decide who to show your video to in the first hour. Getting the hashtags right in that first hour matters more than most creators think.

## Shorts vs regular videos — does the approach differ?

A bit, yes.

For regular long-form videos, your hashtags should match the core topic closely. If your video is about "beginner guitar lessons," your hashtags should stay in that lane. Don't use #music or #entertainment — they're too broad to help.

For Shorts, you can mix in one format hashtag (#Shorts or #YouTubeShorts) alongside your topic hashtags. YouTube's own help pages suggest this for Shorts discovery.

This tool accounts for both formats. Just make sure your input keyword reflects what the video is actually about.

## Common hashtag mistakes to avoid

Using too many. Again — more than 15 and YouTube nullifies all of them.

Using irrelevant ones to chase views. If your video is about personal finance and you tag #gaming, YouTube doesn't reward that. It confuses the algorithm and may suppress the video.

Putting hashtags inside your title. YouTube pulls hashtags from your description. Putting them in the title looks spammy and can hurt CTR.
