## Why extracting hashtags is smarter than guessing

Here's the thing most creators skip: competitor research.

You find a video in your niche that's sitting at 500,000 views. Your video on the same topic got 800. What did they do differently?

Part of that answer is in their hashtags. Not the whole answer — but part of it. And this tool gets you that data in about 10 seconds.

Hashtag extraction is also useful for:

- Figuring out which hashtags top channels in your niche consistently use
- Researching a new niche before you start posting in it
- Seeing how Shorts-specific hashtags are used by creators who are growing fast

It's intelligence gathering. The good kind.

## How to use the YouTube hashtags extractor

**Step 1:** Find a YouTube video you want to research. Copy the full URL from your browser.

**Step 2:** Paste the URL into the extractor and hit the button.

**Step 3:** The tool pulls the hashtags from that video's public description and displays them cleanly.

**Step 4:** Copy the output and use it as a reference for your own hashtag research.

Note: this only works on public videos. Unlisted and private videos won't return results.

## What you get from the extraction

The extractor pulls the hashtags as they appear in the video description. You see the exact tags the creator used — formatted, clickable, and ready to copy.

Some videos use 3. Some use 10. Some use none. That's useful information too. If a video with 2 million views uses zero hashtags, that tells you something about how the algorithm weighted other factors for that content.

## Works with YouTube Shorts

Shorts creators often use a different hashtag mix than long-form creators. The extractor works on both.

Paste a Shorts URL and see the tags they used. Compare a few Shorts that went viral in your niche and you'll start seeing patterns fast.

## Frequently asked questions

**Can I extract hashtags from any YouTube video?**
Any public video, yes. Private and unlisted videos are not accessible.

**Does the tool show hashtags from the description or the title?**
Hashtags in YouTube descriptions. If a creator put hashtags in the title (not recommended), those won't be captured separately.

**Can I use extracted hashtags directly on my own videos?**
Use them as research, not copy-paste. The point is to understand what's working in your niche, then build your own relevant set.

**Does this work for YouTube Shorts?**
Yes, paste any Shorts URL and the extractor pulls the hashtags from the description.

**Is there a limit to how many videos I can research?**
No limit. Run it on as many videos as you need.
