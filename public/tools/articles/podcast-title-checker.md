You've written your podcast episode title. Now the real question: is it actually good?

"Good" means three things: clear enough that someone knows what the episode is about, interesting enough that they want to listen, and search-aware enough that it shows up when the right person is looking.

This tool scores your title across those three dimensions and gives you specific notes on what to improve.

## What the analyzer actually measures

**Clarity score:** Does the title clearly communicate the episode topic? Ambiguous titles lose listeners before they even read the description. "The Power of Habits" scores lower than "How to Build a Daily Exercise Habit You'll Actually Keep."

**Curiosity score:** Does the title create a gap - something the listener wants resolved? A curiosity gap is the distance between what someone knows and what they want to know. Titles that open a gap get more clicks than titles that just describe.

**Search intent score:** Does the title match how someone would actually search for this topic on Spotify, Apple Podcasts, or Google? Natural language keywords, specific phrases, and clear topic signals all improve this score.

## How to use the podcast title analyzer

**Step 1:** Write your episode title - your current best version.

**Step 2:** Paste it into the analyzer tool.

**Step 3:** See your scores across the three dimensions.

**Step 4:** Read the notes. The tool gives you specific, actionable feedback: what's working, what isn't, and suggestions for improvement.

**Step 5:** Edit your title based on the feedback, then run it again to see if the score improves.

Most podcasters run their title through 2-3 iterations before landing on the final version. The analyzer makes that iteration fast.

## Common title problems the analyzer catches

**Too vague:** Titles like "A Conversation with Jane" or "Marketing Strategy Deep Dive" don't tell listeners enough. No clear benefit, no clear topic, no click.

**Too long:** Titles over 80 characters get truncated in most podcast apps. Front-loading the important information matters.

**Too clever:** Wordplay and puns might work for established shows with loyal audiences. For discovery, clarity beats cleverness every time.

**Missing the keyword:** If someone searches "B2B email marketing" and your episode about B2B email marketing is titled "Growing Your Pipeline," they'll never find it.

**Too specific without context:** "The 3-2-1 Method" means nothing without explaining what it's for. A method title needs a benefit attached.

## Why scoring your titles before publishing matters

Here's a simple reality: once an episode is published, most podcast platforms don't show historical episode performance data the same way YouTube does. You can't A/B test podcast titles on Spotify the way you can test YouTube thumbnails.

Getting your title right before publishing is the only optimization window you have.

Spending 5 minutes running your title through this analyzer before every episode is one of the highest-ROI habits a podcaster can build.

## Frequently asked questions

**What's a good score to aim for?**
Look at the individual dimension notes rather than chasing a specific total score. A high clarity score matters more than a high curiosity score for search-driven content. Adjust based on your distribution strategy.

**Can I use this for YouTube video titles too?**
The same principles apply. While the tool is built for podcast episodes, the clarity/curiosity/search framework works for any content title.

**Does this tool tell me the exact keywords to use?**
It gives you signal on search intent direction. For specific keyword data, use the YouTube Keyword Generator tool alongside this one for your distribution research.

**How many times can I run the analyzer?**
As many times as you need. Iterate until you're satisfied.

**Should I prioritize the clarity score or the curiosity score?**
Depends on your growth stage. New shows need clarity more than curiosity - search discovery is the main growth channel. Established shows with loyal audiences can lean into curiosity hooks because subscribers will click regardless.
