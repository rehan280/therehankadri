## How to use the thumbnail downloader

**Step 1:** Copy the YouTube video URL you want to reference.

**Step 2:** Paste it into the tool. The tool parses the video ID automatically.

**Step 3:** Get thumbnail URLs in multiple resolutions: maxresdefault (HD), hqdefault (high quality), mqdefault (medium), and sddefault.

**Step 4:** Open the resolution you need and save the image.

For design reference work, use maxresdefault. It's the full 1280 x 720 version.

## What the resolution options mean

- **maxresdefault:** 1280 x 720. The full HD thumbnail. Not all videos have this — older or lower-quality uploads may not.
- **hqdefault:** 480 x 360. Reliable across all videos. Good for reference.
- **mqdefault:** 320 x 180. Medium quality.
- **sddefault:** 120 x 90. Lowest resolution. Only useful if you're building something size-specific.

For design reference or competitive research, stick with maxresdefault or hqdefault.

## Frequently asked questions

**Can I use downloaded thumbnails on my own videos?**
No. Thumbnails are copyrighted by their creators. Downloaded images are for research reference only, not reuse.

**What if maxresdefault doesn't load for a video?**
Not every video has a maxresdefault version. Use hqdefault as a fallback.

**Does this work for YouTube Shorts thumbnails?**
Yes. Shorts have thumbnails too. Paste the Shorts URL and the tool will parse the thumbnail URLs.

**Can I bulk-download thumbnails from multiple videos?**
The current tool processes one URL at a time. For bulk research, run it multiple times.

**Does the tool require the full YouTube URL or just the video ID?**
Either works. Paste the full URL or just the 11-character video ID.
