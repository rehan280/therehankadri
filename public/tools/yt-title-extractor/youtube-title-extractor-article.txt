H2: YouTube Title Best Practices
Pure value section. Targets adjacent keywords, makes the page a reference resource.
H3: Keep It Under 70 Characters for Search
YouTube allows 100 characters total but truncates titles in search results and suggested feeds around the 70-character mark. Put your main message in the first 70 characters. Use this tool to extract top-ranking titles and check their actual lengths.
H3: Put Your Main Keyword First
YouTube's algorithm reads title keywords left to right. The further right your keyword sits, the weaker the signal. If your topic is "YouTube SEO," your title should start with "YouTube SEO…" not end with it.
H3: Use Numbers
Numbers make titles scannable and set clear expectations. "5 Ways to Grow Your Channel" consistently outperforms "Ways to Grow Your Channel." Extract titles from top videos in your niche — you'll see numbers everywhere.
H3: Use Brackets and Parentheses Strategically
Words in brackets act as secondary descriptors that improve CTR. "(Full Tutorial)", "[2026 Update]", "(Beginner Friendly)" give extra context without eating into the main title. Use them sparingly — one pair maximum.
H3: Write for the Click, Optimize for Search
Your title has two jobs: rank in YouTube search and get people to click. Most creators optimize for one and forget the other. A keyword-stuffed title ranks but gets ignored. A clickbait title gets clicks but ranks for nothing. Balance both.
H3: Never Change a Performing Title
If a video is already getting steady views and impressions, don't touch the title. YouTube's algorithm has already categorized it. Changing the title resets that process and can temporarily drop your rankings.
