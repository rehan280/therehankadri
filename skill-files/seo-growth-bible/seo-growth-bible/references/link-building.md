## THE MODERN LINK BUILDING MINDSET (2026 UPDATE)

Links are still one of the most reliable ranking signals. But the goal has shifted. You're no longer just optimizing for Google PageRank. You're optimizing for **brand presence** — across search, AI, social, and forums.

Google and LLMs (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini) now look at **who mentions you**, **where**, and **in what context**. Getting cited alongside trusted brands in relevant content carries as much weight as the link itself.

**The real goal:** Earn backlinks AND co-citations — train the internet to associate your name with the right topics.

### Why Links Still Matter in 2026
- **Still a ranking factor:** Google still uses links to assess content quality
- **A discovery path:** Links help Googlebot find new pages and understand how they connect
- **An authority indicator:** Links from credible, relevant sites act as third-party endorsements
- **E-E-A-T enhancers:** High-quality links reinforce Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness
- **LLM training signal:** LLMs pull answers from trusted sources; being cited in those sources = AI visibility

### What Is a Co-Citation?
A co-citation happens when your brand is mentioned in the same content as authoritative sources — **even without a hyperlink**.

Examples:
- A blog post listing "top AI marketing tools" that includes your brand next to HubSpot
- A Reddit thread comparing CRM alternatives that mentions your product by name
- A podcast that references your data alongside known experts

These references help LLMs associate your brand with key topics and entities. That builds **contextual authority** — the kind that gets surfaced in AI-generated answers.

### The 3 Dimensions of Link Value
1. **Authority** — Domain Rating (Ahrefs DR) or Domain Authority (Moz DA) or Semrush Authority Score (AS)
2. **Relevance** — How topically related is the linking page to your content?
3. **Traffic** — Does the linking page get real human visitors? (Filter for >100/mo in Ahrefs)

A DR40 link from a relevant, trafficked page beats a DR80 link from an irrelevant zero-traffic page. Every time.

## UNDERSTANDING DOMAIN AUTHORITY / WEBSITE AUTHORITY

"Domain Authority" is not a Google metric. It's a third-party approximation of ranking potential. Each tool calculates it differently.

### Semrush: Authority Score (AS)
Factors: link quality + quantity, estimated organic traffic, spam indicators in the link profile. Unique because it weighs both backlink data AND organic traffic. More holistic than pure backlink scores.

### Moz: Domain Authority (DA)
Factors: number of unique linking domains, total backlinks, authority and relevance of linking domains. Unique domains linking to you is the most important factor for DA.

### Ahrefs: Domain Rating (DR)
Factors: number of unique referring domains, DR of those domains, how many sites the referring domain links out to. Fewer outgoing links from the referring domain = more "link juice" passed to each recipient.

**Important:** The same site will score differently across tools. Backlinko scores 62 (Semrush), 90 (Ahrefs), and 74 (Moz). This is normal — use all three directionally, not as absolute truth.

## THE LINKABLE ASSET FRAMEWORK

Certain content types consistently earn more links than others. These are "linkable assets" — content people want to cite, share, and embed.

### 1. Visual Assets (Charts, Diagrams, Infographics)
When someone embeds your graphic, they credit you with a link. Backlinko's Google CTR chart still earns new backlinks years after publication. Create standalone visual representations of data or processes.

### 2. Original Research & Data Studies
The single highest-leverage linkable format. Journalists and bloggers need data to back their stories. When they cite yours, you earn editorial links from high-DR publications.

**The Data Study Process:**
- **Pick a sharp question:** One timely, specific question your audience cares about. Validate in Google, Reddit, recent news.
- **Design a credible methodology:** Include a "Methods" box — who you surveyed, sample size, collection method, date, definitions, quality checks (bot/duplicate removal).
- **Package for citation:** Pull 8–12 standout stats with source + year. Add simple charts, downloadable data, plain-English captions people can copy and credit.
- **Lead with the angle:** Write the headline and first sentence you want a reporter to quote. Your data must clearly prove that story.
- **Publish and pitch on a hook:** Time release to a trend or calendar moment. Pitch writers who regularly cite data in your niche.

Backlinko's Google Ranking Factors study earned 75,000+ backlinks. Most from sites quoting original stats from the report. Stats pages make up 16% of Backlinko's content but generate 32% of high-quality backlinks.

### 3. Free Tools
Free tools attract links because people naturally share great, free solutions. A calculator, checker, generator, or audit tool can earn thousands of passive backlinks over its lifetime without ongoing outreach. Solve a user problem → links accumulate.

### 4. Ultimate / Definitive Guides
Comprehensive, multi-chapter resources that become the go-to reference in a niche. Done right, they earn links and citations for years. Important caveat: **more is not always better**. A massive guide trying to satisfy every intent can be too broad to rank or too overwhelming to use. Guides aligned to a clear, specific intent (beginner 101, tactical playbook, deep technical walkthrough) perform better in both search and AI citations.

### 5. Statistics / Data Roundup Pages
Lists of curated stats on a topic. Reporters and bloggers searching for quick-reference data link to these constantly. Backlinko built 50+ stats pages after a backlink audit revealed they generated the second-highest number of referring domains of any content type.

### 6. Shoulder Niche Content
For sites in boring niches: one study found "tangential content" (not directly related to what a site sells) resulted in 30% more links and 77% more social shares vs. industry-specific content. Cast wider on topics adjacent to your core niche.

## THE SKYSCRAPER TECHNIQUE (Brian Dean's Original Method)

The Skyscraper Technique generated a 110% traffic increase in 14 days when Brian Dean first executed it (April 2013). His Google Ranking Factors post gained 2M+ referral visitors from it.

### The 3 Steps

**Step 1: Find Proven Linkable Assets**
Find content that already generates tons of links — don't guess, verify. Use Ahrefs to find top-linked pages in your niche. These are proven link magnets. Your goal: find the "tallest skyscraper" in your space.

**Step 2: Make Something Even Better**
Four ways to upgrade:
- **Make it longer:** If the best list has 50 items, publish 150 or 500. More items = more comprehensive = harder to top.
- **Make it more up-to-date:** Old content with outdated stats or missing modern developments is easy to outclass. Update with cutting-edge information.
- **Make it better designed:** Visually superior content earns more links and shares vs. identical content on an ugly page. Add banners, structured chapters, sticky TOC, custom graphics.
- **Make it more thorough:** Turn bland bullet lists into actual explanations. Add depth, context, and actionable guidance under each point.

**Step 3: Reach Out to the Right People**
Target sites already linking to the inferior version. They've already proven interest in this topic. Your pitch: "There's a better version of what you've linked to."

## FINDING HIGH-QUALITY SITES TO PITCH

### Identify Authoritative, Topically Relevant Sites
Search your topic in Google and Google News. Note publishers that cover it regularly. Add industry associations, trade journals, and reputable blogs to a seed list. Then pull competitor backlink reports using Semrush Backlink Analytics — look at referring domains for their best non-commercial content.

**Google search operators:**
```
"[topic]" inurl:links site:.edu
"[topic]" resources
"[topic]" "helpful resources"
"[topic]" "write for us"
"[topic]" "guest post guidelines"
"[topic]" "become a contributor"
```

### Check Authority and Traffic
Use Semrush or Ahrefs to check Authority Score and organic traffic. Target sites with real rankings and consistent traffic. Real organic visibility matters more than high DA scores.

### Check Outbound Link Quality
Scan a few recent posts. If you see casino/CBD/loan farms, obvious paid lists, or spun content → skip it. Quality sites have high editorial standards.

### Check for Nofollow
Right-click → "View page source." Look for `rel="nofollow"` on the link. If present: nofollow. If absent: dofollow. You want dofollow links whenever possible.

### Avoid These Red Flags
- Sites with mostly guest posts (Google has largely devalued guest post links)
- Sites that accept any submission without editorial review
- Low organic traffic despite high DA
- Links to irrelevant, thin, or spammy content

### Build Segmented Prospect Lists
Organize prospects into buckets: journalists, bloggers, industry sites, niche forums, media outlets. Tailor your pitch for each.
- Journalists need quick stats and quotes
- Bloggers want a unique angle
- Industry sites look for data-driven resources

### AI Prospecting Prompt
```
You're a link prospecting assistant. For the topic [your topic] in [your niche], find 30 relevant,
high-authority prospects across:
1. Journalists who recently covered it (with 2-3 article links)
2. Resource pages
3. Listicles that cite stats
4. Sites that publish expert roundups with external links

Output a table with: Site | Section | Why they link | Example URL | Est. Authority Score | Contact Path
Prioritize recency (<12 months) and topical relevance.
```

## CRAFTING THE OUTREACH EMAIL (AIDA Framework)

Focus on 100–150 highly targeted, personalized pitches per campaign. Quality over volume.

**AIDA Applied:**
- **Attention:** Personalized hook. Reference a recent article, tweet, or podcast they published.
- **Interest:** What makes your content unique? New data? Fresh angle? More comprehensive resource?
- **Desire:** Tie your content back to their audience. Point out a stat or graphic they could easily use. Make clear what's in it for them.
- **Action:** End with ONE specific ask. Not "share, link, or let me know" — pick one action like "consider adding this to your piece."

**When pitching first-party data:** Lead with the single most surprising finding. Give them the quotable sentence. Make it trivial for a journalist to cite your research.

## STRATEGY 1: BECOME A SOURCE FOR REPORTERS AND BLOGGERS

The highest-quality links come from journalists citing expert sources. You don't need a PR agency. You need to be helpful to the right people at the right time.

**The Three Tools:**
- **Featured.com** (freemium) — Reporters post questions, you answer, they quote and link you
- **Help a Reporter Out / Connectively** (free) — The original HARO, now rebranded
- **MentionMatch / Help a B2B Writer** (free) — Newer alternative gaining traction

**The HARO/Featured Workflow:**
1. Register as a source
2. Set email filters for your category (business, tech, health, etc.)
3. Screen queries — only respond to DR >50 publications, queries directly in your expertise, queries where you can add a genuinely unique perspective
4. Respond within 1–4 hours of delivery (early responses win)
5. Keep responses 100–200 words unless more is requested
6. Format: one-sentence credential → insight #1 → insight #2 → quotable line → name/title/company/URL

**Expected success rate:** 5–20% of quality responses get published
**Time to first link:** 2–6 weeks
**DR range:** 50–90 (journalists work for major publications)

Greg Heilers of Jolly SEO has sent 200,000+ pitches. These platforms consistently produce the best link-building results of any strategy at scale.

## STRATEGY 2: DIGITAL PR AND DATA-DRIVEN LINK BUILDING

Original research earns passive links for years. Target: editorial links from DR70+ publications (Forbes, TechCrunch, industry trade journals).

**The Data Study Formula:**
1. **Find a data gap** — Search "[your niche] statistics" — what data is old or missing?
2. **Collect data via:** Survey (500+ respondents), web scraping, proprietary product data, API pulls from public datasets (Google Trends, government databases)
3. **Turn data into 5–10 shareable findings** — counterintuitive or surprising stats work best
4. **Create visual assets** — infographic, chart series, interactive visualization
5. **Publish on a dedicated, linkable page** — not buried inside a blog post
6. **Pitch journalists with personalized angles** for their specific beat

**Outreach Angles:**
- "[Surprising finding] challenges common wisdom about [topic]"
- "New data reveals [trend] in [industry] — implications for [journalist's audience]"
- "We analyzed [X data points] and found [counterintuitive finding]"

## STRATEGY 3: GUEST POSTING (THE RIGHT WAY)

Guest posting has weakened as a tactic because Google has largely devalued guest post links at scale. But strategic, high-quality placements on genuinely selective publications still work.

**Rules for Guest Posts That Still Work:**
- Only post on sites that are very picky about what they publish
- Don't use exact match anchor text in external links
- Focus on topically relevant sites even if that means lower DA
- Never rely on guest posting as your sole link building strategy

**Minimum qualifying standards:**
- DR ≥ 30 (Ahrefs)
- Organic traffic ≥ 500/mo
- Topically relevant
- Real author bylines (not anonymous)
- Dofollow body content links (not bio-only)

**The Pitch Template:**
```
Subject: Guest Post Idea: [Specific, Compelling Headline]

Hi [Editor Name],

I've been reading [publication] — your recent piece on [specific article] was particularly useful.

I'd love to contribute to your readers: "[Proposed Headline]"

The article would cover:
• [Key point 1 — specific benefit for their audience]
• [Key point 2]
• [Key point 3]

I've previously published at [Publication 1] and [Publication 2]. Here are samples: [links]

Happy to adjust the angle if you have something in mind.

[Name]
```

**Writing for Acceptance:**
- Write for THEIR audience, not a generic piece
- Follow their style guide exactly
- Include 1–2 links to their own existing content
- Your link should appear naturally in context, not forced into the conclusion
- Over-deliver: submit with custom images if possible

## STRATEGY 4: BROKEN LINK BUILDING

You're doing webmasters a favor AND offering a replacement — the most naturally motivated outreach in link building.

**Step 1 — Find Broken Link Targets**

Method A (Ahrefs):
1. Enter competitor URL → Site Explorer → Best by links
2. Filter: HTTP code 404
3. Use Backlinks report to find who links to each dead page

Method B (Screaming Frog):
1. Crawl resource pages: `[niche] "resources"` or `[niche] "helpful links"`
2. Check External Links → filter 404s
3. Find who links to 404s via Ahrefs batch analysis

**Step 2 — Match or Create Replacement Content**
Do you have content covering the same topic? If yes: proceed. If no: create it first.

**Outreach Template:**
```
Subject: Broken link on [their page title]

Hi [Name],

I was browsing your [topic] resource page and noticed a broken link:
[Dead URL] → returns a 404 error

I recently published a piece that might make a good replacement:
[Your URL]

Thought it worth flagging — broken links hurt user experience.

[Name]
```
**Expected response rate:** 5–15%

## STRATEGY 5: LINK RECLAMATION

### Unlinked Brand Mention Recovery
**Find mentions:**
- Ahrefs: Alerts → Mentions → your brand → filter: no link
- Google: `"[your brand name]" -site:yourdomain.com`
- Mention.com or Brand24 for real-time monitoring

**Prioritize:** DR >30, body content mentions, topically relevant pages

**Outreach:**
```
Hi [Name], thank you for mentioning [Brand] in your piece on [topic].
I noticed the mention isn't linked — would you mind adding a link?
[Your URL]
Thanks!
```
**Expected conversion:** 20–40% (easiest link building outreach)

### Lost Link Recovery
Use Ahrefs "Lost Backlinks" report (filter: last 30–90 days).
1. Your URL changed? → 301 redirect immediately
2. They updated their content? → Reach out and ask for re-inclusion
3. Their page was deleted? → Move on

## STRATEGY 7: REVERSE OUTREACH (STATS PAGES)

Create pages that act as magnets — links come to you without active outreach.

Build high-quality statistics pages on topics reporters search for daily. If your page ranks #1–3 for "[topic] statistics," you earn links passively.

**Structure of a winning stats page:**

## STRATEGY 9: TIERED LINK BUILDING

Tiered links amplify the power of existing backlinks by building links to your linking pages — not directly to your site.

**Structure:**
- Tier 1: High-quality links pointing to your site (guest posts, digital PR, HARO)
- Tier 2: Mid-quality links pointing to Tier 1 pages (amplifies their authority)
- Tier 3: Social signals pointing to Tier 2 pages

**White-hat execution:**
1. Publish guest post on Site A → Tier 1 link
2. Promote Site A's page on social → Tier 3 signals
3. Get another guest post on Site B linking to Site A's post → Tier 2
4. Site A gains authority → passes more juice to your site

**Never:** Use automated tools, PBNs, or paid link farms for Tier 2/3. Manual Google penalties still exist.

## HOW TO CONDUCT A BACKLINK AUDIT

Do one quarterly and always after major site changes or migrations.

### Why Regular Audits Matter
- Identify authoritative vs. low-quality links
- Optimize anchor text distribution
- Detect unusual spikes in toxic links before penalties hit
- Find and reclaim lost links
- Discover which content types earn the most referring domains
- Find competitor backlink gaps to target

### Backlink Audit Tools
- **Semrush Backlink Audit** — Full link profile, toxicity score, built-in disavow tool
- **Ahrefs Site Explorer** — Best for lost/new links tracking, competitor comparison
- **Moz Link Explorer** — DA scores, spam score, link analysis
- **Google Search Console** — Free; shows Google's actual index of links to your site
- **Majestic SEO** — Trust Flow and Citation Flow, strong historical data

### Step-by-Step Audit Process

**Step 1 — Export Full Backlink Profile**
Pull from both Ahrefs/Semrush AND Google Search Console. Different tools find different links. Combine them.

**Step 2 — Assess Anchor Text Distribution**
A healthy profile looks like:
- ~40–50% brand anchors ("your brand name," "author name")
- ~20–30% generic anchors ("click here," "read more," "this article")
- ~10–20% partial match (contains some of target keyword)
- ~5–10% exact match (target keyword verbatim)
- ~5% naked URLs

**Red flag:** >20% exact match anchor = over-optimization risk.

Backlinko's audit found 17.4% of their backlinks use branded anchors — this builds brand authority AND improves SEO.

**Step 3 — Identify Link Types and Sources**
Check % of text vs. image links, dofollow vs. nofollow, unique domains vs. repeat domains.

**Step 4 — Spot Toxic Links**
Signals of toxic links:
- Zero organic traffic on the linking domain
- Site publishes exclusively thin/duplicate content
- Site is completely unrelated to your niche
- Mass link placement (same site linking 50+ times)
- High spam score (Moz) or high toxicity score (Semrush)

**Step 5 — Disavow When Necessary**
Use Google's Disavow Tool in Search Console. Format:
```
# Disavow file — [Your site] — [Date]
domain:spammydomain.com
https://specificspampage.com/post
```
Only disavow genuinely harmful links. Over-disavowing is dangerous.

**Step 6 — Identify What Earns Links**
Which posts/pages attract the most referring domains? Build more of that content type.

In one Backlinko audit, statistics roundup pages earned the second-highest number of backlinks from referring domains. This insight led to the creation of 50+ new stats pages.

**Step 7 — Find Competitor Gaps**
Use Ahrefs Link Intersect: find domains linking to competitors but not you → prioritize as outreach targets.

### Critical Case: Site Migration
One SEO consultant's client lost 50% of backlinks overnight due to URL structure change after migration. A backlink audit revealed all 404 pages. Setting up proper redirects nearly doubled the site's traffic (3K → 6K/mo) in under 3 months. Always audit before and after any URL structure change.

## LINK BUILDING SERVICES: WHAT TO USE AND WHAT TO AVOID

### Services/Approaches to AVOID
1. **Fiverr link packages** — "500 backlinks for $5" = link farms, PBNs, or automated spam
2. **PBN (Private Blog Network) links** — Google detects them; manual penalty + de-indexation risk
3. **Paid links without rel="sponsored"** — Against Google guidelines; high penalty risk
4. **Link exchanges at scale** — Systematic A→B→A swapping is a detectable footprint
5. **Donation links** — Google has classified these as "paid links"
6. **Generic directory submissions** — Zero value post-Penguin
7. **Press release syndication for links** — Near-zero link value; use only for awareness
8. **Footer/sitewide links** — Appears on every page; triggers unnatural link signals
9. **Blog comment links** — Rarely dofollow; use only for relationship-building, never for link value

### Services/Approaches That Work
1. **Digital PR agencies with editorial relationships** — Pitch original research to real journalists at real publications
2. **Featured.com / HARO / Connectively** — Source matchmaking platforms (DIY or via VA)
3. **Niche edits/link insertions** — Only from reputable agencies with genuine editorial relationships
4. **Podcast guest appearances** — Show notes usually include a backlink; builds links AND brand authority
5. **Sponsored content with rel="sponsored"** — Compliant with Google guidelines when properly tagged

**The Rule of Thumb:** If a service promises volume at low cost — it's building links that will eventually hurt you. Quality link building is expensive in either time or money. There is no shortcut.

## BEGINNER QUICK-WIN TACTICS

Low-effort, lower-impact starting points for sites with no link profile:

- **Ask people you know** — Friends, colleagues, clients. Only if topically relevant.
- **Alumni directories** — Many university sites list alumni businesses. Worth checking.
- **Chamber of Commerce** — A nearly guaranteed link waiting to happen.
- **Company/niche directories** — Focus on highly-relevant industry directories only (not generic web directories).
- **Email people you mention** — Whenever you cite someone, let them know. Some will link back.
- **Crowdsourced post contributions** — Always accept. 5–10 minutes per contribution, real link from a real site.
- **Event resources** — A resource page for conference attendees often earns passive links from that event's community.
- **RSS feed with internal links** — Even scraped content will copy your internal links, giving passive link distribution.

6. **Irrelevant directory submissions** — Zero value post-Penguin
7. **Press release syndication for links** — Near-zero link value
8. **Buying cheap link packages** — Fiverr and similar = link farms = future penalty
9. **Aggressive exact-match guest post anchor text** — Use branded or partial match instead
10. **Ignoring disavow** — A sudden spike in toxic links left unaddressed can trigger a manual review

## OUTREACH MASTERY — AHREFS + BACKLINKO PRINCIPLES (2026)

### The Outreach Numbers Reality
Average link outreach conversion rate: **1–5%** (surveyed from 800+ practitioners on X and LinkedIn).

Run the math:
- 100 emails × 1% = 1 link
- 1,000 emails × 1% = 10 links

You need volume AND quality. Not one at the expense of the other.

### The Scoped Shotgun Approach (Ahrefs)
The best of both worlds:
- Use templates (for scale)
- Add dynamic personalization variables (for relevance)
- Tools: Pitchbox, Buzzstream
- This avoids generic spam while not requiring a fully custom email for every prospect

### The #1 Rule: Make It About Them
Most outreach fails because it's about the sender, not the recipient. Turn this around:
- Lead with something specific about their work (not templated praise)
- Explain exactly why your content is useful for THEIR audience
- Only share something genuinely unique and valuable
- If your content isn't unique, don't pitch it

**Bad flattery (delete immediately):**
"I just read your article and it is very cool. I'm a long-time reader of your blog and I think your content is totally amazing."

**Good specificity:**
"I saw your post on [specific topic] — you mentioned [specific point]. I just published a guide that covers [specific thing they didn't address], which your readers might find useful."

### When to Do Outreach (Only for Your Best Content)
Don't do outreach for every post — it burns bridges. Use the Ahrefs "Top Content" report (sorted by social power) to identify posts worth promoting. Only pitch your top 10–20% of content.

### Finding Prospects for Outreach
4 main methods:
1. **Linkers of similar content** — Pull referring domains from competing articles in Ahrefs → outreach with your superior version
2. **Topical authors** — Search Google for your topic, note who writes about it repeatedly
3. **Mentionees** — If your post mentions other bloggers, let them know
4. **Advanced operators** — Use search operators to find super-relevant posts that don't mention your angle yet (gives you a ready-made hook)

### Guest Post Outreach
- Find the managing editor / head of content (check "Team" or "About" page, or LinkedIn)
- Use Hunter.io to find email addresses
- Read the site's guest post guidelines before pitching
- Pitch with an outline — it shows seriousness and saves back-and-forth
- Your pitch should: explain what you want to write, who you are, and why their audience will benefit

### The Link Outreach Template (Skyscraper / Broken Link / Resource Page)
```
Subject: [Their article title] — quick note

Hi [Name],

I noticed you [linked to / mentioned] [Original Article/Resource] in your post on [their topic].

I just published [brief description of what makes yours better/different]: [Your URL]

[One specific reason it helps their readers.]

Either way, keep up the great work.

[Your name]
```

**Key elements:**
- Under 100 words
- One specific differentiator (not a list of features)
- No "Would you mind linking to it?" — let them decide
- Personalized subject line (not "Great post!" or "Link opportunity")

### The "Relationship First" Approach
Build relationships before you ask for links. Ways to warm up prospects:
- Share their content on social media
- Leave a thoughtful comment
- Quote them in your posts (then let them know)
- Engage with them on Twitter/X or LinkedIn
People do favors for friends, not strangers. A few weeks of genuine engagement dramatically lifts reply rates.

### Naming Your Strategies = Passive Links
Named tactics become self-perpetuating link magnets:
- Brian Dean's "Skyscraper Technique" — referenced in thousands of blog posts, automatically cited
- "The Moving Man Method" — earns ongoing links and LLM citations because people ask for it by name

## LINK BUILDING IN THE AI ERA — CO-CITATIONS & BRAND MENTIONS

### The New Link Building Goal (2026)
Links still matter for Google rankings. But the goal has expanded: you're now optimizing for **brand presence** across the entire web — traditional backlinks, forum mentions, expert quotes in articles, and social proof signals that AI systems use.

Backlinko has earned 1.5 million+ backlinks. But the way they think about it has changed: context beats the `<href>` tag. A brand mention without a link can be as valuable as a link for AI visibility.

### Co-Citations: The New Non-Link Signal
A co-citation is when your brand is mentioned in the same content as authoritative sources — even without a hyperlink. Three examples:
- Blog post listing "top AI marketing tools" that includes your brand next to HubSpot or Semrush
- Reddit thread comparing CRM alternatives that names your product
- YouTube video or podcast that references your insights alongside known experts

These references help LLMs associate your brand with key topics and entities, building contextual authority that gets surfaced in AI-generated answers.

### Where to Build Co-Citations
Priority platforms for LLM training data:
1. Reddit (subreddits relevant to your product/service category)
2. Quora (detailed expert answers)
3. LinkedIn (professional content and commentary)
4. YouTube (video descriptions and transcripts)
5. High-authority editorial: Forbes, Business Insider, TechRadar, NerdWallet, CNBC
6. Product review platforms: G2, Capterra, Trustpilot, Amazon

### The Guest Post Evolution
Old goal: get a followed link from DR40+ site.
New goal: get a meaningful brand mention in trusted, relevant content that influences AI recommendations.

"Guest content" now includes:
- Traditional guest posts (link-focused)
- Being listed in "best alternatives" or "top X" posts
- Substack newsletters mentioning your brand to their audience
- Tutorial posts that use your tool as part of their solution
- Affiliate content featuring your product alongside competing options

Strategy: Use guest content to expand the "surface area" of your brand in meaningful contexts.

### STRATEGY 10: GUESTOGRAPHICS

A hybrid of infographic + guest post invented by Brian Dean. Results: one case study showed +175% organic traffic.

**How it works:**
1. Create a high-quality infographic on a topic your target audience cares about
2. Find sites that have written about the same topic (search your topic in Google)
3. Pitch them: "I have an infographic on this topic. I'll write a unique 200–300 word intro for your audience if you publish it."
4. Include embed code — when they publish it, they link back to the original infographic on your site

**Why it works:** You remove all friction. The publisher gets custom content AND a visual asset. You get a contextual backlink. No cold pitch needed for a standalone link.

**Best targets:** Blogs that publish frequently but have no graphic design resources. Niche bloggers in B2B, health, finance, marketing.

### STRATEGY 11: PODCAST APPEARANCES

Brian Dean: "Podcasts are the new guest posts." And in some ways they're better.

- Scales: once you have a few appearances, more come inbound

**Pitch approach:** Short email, lead with relevance to their audience, include 2–3 specific episode topic ideas, keep under 100 words.

### STRATEGY 16: THE MOVING MAN METHOD

Find outdated resources — companies that changed their name, moved their URL, or shut down — then reach out to sites still linking to the dead resource.

**3-step process:**
1. Find outdated resources:
   - Companies that rebranded or shut down
   - Resources that changed URL
   - Old tools or services that no longer exist
2. Find everyone still linking to the old/dead URL (Ahrefs → "Broken Backlinks" or search the old URL in Site Explorer)
3. Email those sites: "Hey, just wanted to let you know that [resource] no longer exists. I have an updated, similar resource here: [your URL]. Might be worth an update."

**Why it works:** You're helping them fix their site. It's in their interest, not just yours. High response and conversion rates.

**Real result from Backlinko:** "The Moving Man Method" earned thousands of links — and then even more because the named tactic itself became a cited framework.

### STRATEGY 22: REVERSE ENGINEER COMPETITOR BACKLINKS

The single most reliable way to find link opportunities in your niche is to find the exact pages that link to your competitors.

**Step-by-step:**
1. Put 3–5 competitors into Ahrefs Site Explorer
2. Go to "Backlinks" → filter: DR >30, organic traffic >100, not homepage
3. Export to CSV — this is your outreach list
4. Look for patterns: what types of pages link to them most? (Resource pages? Guest posts? Press coverage?)
5. Prioritize the easiest pattern first

**The Competitor Content Angle:**
1. Find a competitor page with 50+ referring domains
2. Create something definitively better (Skyscraper)
3. Pitch everyone who links to the competitor
